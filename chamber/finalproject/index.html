Welcome to Cayos Cochinos

Cayos Cochinos is a hidden gem off the northern coast of Honduras, just 30 km from La Ceiba. This small archipelago, part of the Atlántida Department, is a protected marine reserve famous for its crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and stunning natural landscapes. The two main islands, Cayo Menor and Cayo Grande, along with several smaller islets, offer a peaceful escape where visitors can experience the rich Garífuna culture, snorkel among colorful fish, relax on pristine beaches, and explore lush tropical surroundings. Perfect for eco-friendly and adventurous travelers.

Explore the Islands

Flora and Fauna

The islands are home to vibrant coral reefs, tropical fish, and rare bird species. Visitors often spot sea turtles, rays, and other marine life while snorkeling or diving in the crystal-clear waters surrounding Cayos Cochinos.

Activities

Explore pristine beaches, swim in turquoise waters, or join guided snorkeling and diving tours to experience the incredible marine life of Cayos Cochinos. Kayaking, hiking, and beachcombing are also popular ways to enjoy the islands.

Conservation and Responsible Tourism

We are committed to preserving the natural environment of Cayos Cochinos. Guests are encouraged to follow eco-friendly practices and respect local wildlife and communities, ensuring that these islands remain a paradise for generations to come.

Did You Know?

  • Cayos Cochinos consists of 2 main islands and 13 small cayes.
  • The area is home to the rare pink boa (Boa constrictor imperator).
  • It is one of the top snorkeling destinations in Central America.
